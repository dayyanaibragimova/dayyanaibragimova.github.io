    ITALY

    Venice

  • Tips

    • As a college kid on a budget, during my study abroad travels I found a lot of free walking tours to go on. The Venice Free Walking Tour was very informational and is one of the best ones that I have gone on. The best part is that it is absolutely free, just make sure to tip the tour guide!

    SPAIN

    Barcelona

  • Eats

    • Breakfast-My favorite breakfast place that I visted has to be Galeria Cosmo. It has a delicous menu (especially coffee lattes), and the interior very cute and trendy. The food is also local and natural.

    Dinner-To be completely honest, I do not remember a single place that really stood out to me, because they are all good. My general advice is to find local places. Paella is my go to!

  • Places to Stay

    • I really enjoyed my stay at the Bicycle Apartments. They are located close to city center, have fully equipped kitchens, and even a washer and dryer! If you are doing a long trip, this is the perfect place and has anything you need. You can use my name in the special field for 10% off (this is not sponsored, just something they promote for friends and family of past guests).

    Granada

  • Eats

    • I studied abroad in Granada during my Bachelor's. Granada offers a lot of authentic Southern-Spanish cousine! You cannot go wrong with basically anything.

    Breakfast/Coffee Break-Cafe 4 Gatos - delicious toastadas. I_Need Coffee - a little on the pricier side but really good coffee and toast.

    Dinner/Tapas- Bar Poe - super yummy tapas.

    Bodrum

  • Eats
  • Places to Stay

    • I stayed at the Doria Hotel and loved it. It is one of a few hotels in Bodrum with a gym, which is a huge perk! The hotel was very clean and the staff was friendly and nice. The best part was that they had a shuttle that drove guests to and from a private beach!
